Scope

This Policy applies to personal information we process as a controller when you interact with our Services, including when you browse our site, create an account, chat with our WhatsApp/NLP assistant, visit our mobile clinics, receive services from health agents, or use telehealth features. Separate terms may apply to clinicians, employees, contractors, and research participants.

Information we collect

Information you provide

  • Account details (name, contact information, password, demographics).
  • Patient information for care (e.g., pregnancy, maternal, neonatal, paediatric health history, vitals, lab orders/results, care plans, symptoms, medications, allergies).
  • Telehealth interactions and messages (including through chat/WhatsApp and in-app forms).
  • Payment, subscription and logistics details (addresses for deliveries or home visits, preferred schedules).
  • Support requests, survey responses, and newsletter sign‑ups.

Information we collect automatically

  • Usage data and analytics (pages viewed, device identifiers, app telemetry, crash logs).
  • Approximate location (from IP) and, with permission, precise location for home visits or deliveries.
  • Cookies and similar technologies for authentication, security, and preferences.

Information from third parties

  • Referrals from clinicians and partner facilities.
  • Laboratory providers and logistics partners (status updates, results, proof of delivery).
  • Identity or eligibility verification providers, where permitted.

Sensitive health data. Some of the information we process relates to health and may be considered special category data. We process such data only as necessary to deliver care and operate the Services, with appropriate safeguards and, where required, your consent.

How we use information

  • Provide, personalize, and improve the Services (including triage, risk stratification, care coordination, and decision support for clinicians).
  • Schedule and deliver home/community visits, mobile clinic services, telehealth consultations, and logistics (DawaXpress).
  • Communicate with you about appointments, results, safety notices, and updates.
  • Operate safety, quality assurance, audit, and training programs.
  • Research and analytics, including de‑identified or aggregated insights to improve maternal, neonatal, and paediatric care.
  • Detect, prevent, and respond to fraud, abuse, security incidents, and legal requests.
  • Comply with applicable laws and professional standards, and establish or defend legal claims.

Legal bases (where applicable)

Where data protection laws require a legal basis (e.g., GDPR/UK GDPR and similar laws), we rely on one or more of the following: (i) consent; (ii) performance of a contract; (iii) compliance with a legal obligation; (iv) vital interests of you or another person; and/or (v) our legitimate interests in delivering and improving safe and accessible healthcare. For special category health data, we rely on your explicit consent, provision of healthcare by professionals bound by confidentiality, and/or public interest in the area of public health, as permitted by law.

Sharing and disclosure

  • Care teams & partners: With clinicians, laboratories, pharmacies, logistics and payment providers involved in your care or requested services.
  • Vendors: With service providers that host infrastructure, provide analytics, messaging, support, or similar processing on our behalf under contractual safeguards.
  • Authorities: When required by law, court order, or to protect health, safety, or rights.
  • Business transfers: In connection with a merger, acquisition, or asset transfer, with appropriate protections.
  • De‑identified data: We may share aggregated or de‑identified information that cannot reasonably be used to identify you.

International transfers

We operate across multiple countries. Your information may be transferred to and processed in locations where we or our service providers operate. We take steps to ensure appropriate protections for such transfers in accordance with applicable law.

Retention

We keep personal information for as long as needed to provide the Services, comply with legal and clinical record‑keeping obligations, resolve disputes, and enforce agreements. Retention periods depend on the type of data, the nature of the Services, and legal requirements in Rwanda, Zambia, Zimbabwe, and any other relevant jurisdictions.

Your choices & rights

  • Access, correction, deletion, or portability of certain personal information.
  • Object to or restrict certain processing, and withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.
  • Manage cookies and communications preferences (e.g., newsletters, product updates).

To exercise rights, contact us at privacy@dawa-health.com or the addresses listed above. We may need to verify your identity and may be unable to fully comply with a request where legal or clinical record‑keeping obligations require retention.

Children’s privacy

Our Services include maternal, neonatal, and paediatric care delivered by trained professionals. We collect and process minors’ information only with appropriate authorization from a parent or legal guardian and as necessary to provide care in accordance with applicable law.

Security

We implement administrative, technical, and physical safeguards designed to protect personal information, including access controls, encryption in transit and at rest where appropriate, secure development practices, and staff training. No system is completely secure; we encourage you to use strong passwords, enable device protections, and notify us immediately of any suspected unauthorized access.

Research & de‑identification

Where we use information for research or public health purposes, we seek to minimize the use of identifiable data, apply de‑identification or pseudonymization where possible, and follow applicable ethics and regulatory requirements.

Cookies & similar technologies

We use cookies and similar technologies to keep you signed in, remember preferences, measure performance, and improve the Services. You can control cookies through your browser settings; disabling certain cookies may affect core functionality.

Third‑party links & services

Our Services may include links to third‑party websites, apps, or integrations (for example, messaging, payments, logistics, or laboratory providers). We are not responsible for their privacy practices. We encourage you to review their policies.

Changes to this Policy
